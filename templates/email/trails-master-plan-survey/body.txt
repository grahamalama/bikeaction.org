**Philly Bike Action suggested responses:**

* *Q: Are there factors that keep you from using trails more often? Select all that apply.*  
    * *Answer:* **Gaps in the trail network, especially the Delaware River Trail, and a lack of high-quality protected bike lanes connecting directly to the trails.**  
* *Q: Which should be a higher priority for the City of Philadelphia?*  
    * *Answer:* **Expanding the trail network (building new trails)** *or* **Both are equally important**  
* *Q: In which parts of Philadelphia would you like to see more trails? Select all that apply.*  
    * *Answer:* **Northeast** (+ elsewhere you care about)  
* *Q: What else would you like us to know as we plan the future of Philadelphia's trails?*  
    * *Answer:* **Prioritize the DRT Lehigh–Allegheny gap. Build separated bike lanes on Richmond St. Also, install traffic calming and wayfinding along E Thompson St and Belgrade St as a lower-traffic alternative to Richmond St.**  
* *Q: What questions do you have?*  
    * *Answer:* **How will the City prioritize the rapid build-out of protected on-street bike connections to the trail network? What will the City do to systemically allocate street space toward protected bike facilities?**

**Why the Lehigh–Allegheny segment?**  
The DRT has the potential to be the safest and most direct connection between Center City and the Northeast—but only if the infrastructure is continuous. This segment is a critical link in making that vision a reality.
